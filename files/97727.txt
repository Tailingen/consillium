The Effectiveness of a Patient Self-reported Pain Scoring Tool and a Satisfaction Survey on Cancer Pain Management
The purpose of this survey is to investigate the usefulness of a patient self-reported scoring tool on patient satisfaction improvement.

BEHAVIORAL: No intervention
Patient satisfaction with the control of cancer-related pain, Patients will answer on a 7-point scale how satisfied they feel about their cancer pain treatment. The scale scores range from 1 (not at all satisfied) to 7 (very much satisfied)., At baseline
Patient perception of pain, Patients will answer on an 11-point scale how much pain they feel. The scales scores range from 0 (no pain at all) to 10 (pain as bad as you can imagine)., At baseline|Patient pain assessment by medical staff, The patient's physician will mark on an 11-point scale the intensity of the patient pain.
